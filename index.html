Three Pieces of Art

Wolf in Sheep Clothing

By: Justin Lee Williams

In this image a wolf is wearing the skin of a sheep, presumably to disguise itself into a crowd of other sheep. The wolf uses its appearance in order to trick sheep, when the reality is that the wolf is actually trying to hunt them. This art piece tells people that even though a person may look like one of your own, it is not wise to trust them immediately. This idiom originated from a book in the New Testament in which it says to not trust false prophets wearing sheep's clothing.

Relativity

By: Maurits Cornelis Escher

This art piece is a more abstract perspective on appearance vs. reality. The lithograph depicts various people going up or down flights of stairs and more. However, the direction of gravitational pull is different for each group of people. This helps portray the theme of appearance and reality, you may view a set of stairs normally and then realize that there is another person using those same set of stairs much more differently. I think this tells us that theres more than one side to anything whether it's a person or thing.

Rubin Vase

By: Edgar Rubin

This image can vary from person to person at first glance. This illusion tests whether or not you can see the vase the middle or the two faces on the left and right. At first, it may be difficult to notice the faces if you noticed the vase first, or it may be hard to notice the vase if you saw the two faces first. This piece has the same recurrent theme as the other art pieces, you may believe something to be a certiant way but there can be a whole other side that you wont notice at a first glance.

Text-to-World



The Watergate Scandal

Before President Richard Nixon's re-election into his second term, five burglars attempted to wiretap the Democratic National Committee (DNC) headquarters at Watergate on June 17, 1972. Four of the five burglars were CIA and the other was working as a security chief for Nixon, which drew suspicion to him. At first Nixon would deny the accusations, but it was later revealed that Richard Nixon ordered the five burglars to wiretap the DNC to get information on the Democratic Party nominee. Nixon may have appeared to be a honest president, but he was willing to lie and commit crime to ensure that he got re-elected.

Text-to-Text

In the novel Holes, there is a character whose nickname is Zero who is different than what he seems to be. In the book, children who have commited crimes are sent to a detention camp called Camp Green Lake, where they are supposed to dig holes that are five feet wide and deep to build character. It is revealed that Zero does not know how to read or write, the main character Stanley offers to teach him in exchange for digging a portion of his hole everyday. At first it may seem that Zero is very cold and distant, but opens up to Stanley over the course of the book and reveals his generous and kind nature.

Poetry Piece

Sonnet 130

By: William Shakespeare

My mistress’ eyes are nothing like the sun;

Coral is far more red than her lips’ red;

If snow be white, why then her breasts are dun;

If hairs be wires, black wires grow on her head.

I have seen roses damask’d, red and white,

But no such roses see I in her cheeks;

And in some perfumes is there more delight

Than in the breath that from my mistress reeks.

I love to hear her speak, yet well I know

That music hath a far more pleasing sound;

I grant I never saw a goddess go;

My mistress, when she walks, treads on the ground:

And yet, by heaven, I think my love as rare

As any she belied with false compare.

In this poem, the speaker attempts to compare their love to a goddess. The speaker talks about physical appearance, the first impression that you get from a person. In comparison, the speakers lover seems imperfect, but the speaker says that their love is a rare form of love. I think that this type of love is unconditional love. The speaker acknowledges their own lovers imperfections, however the speaker insinuates that he still loves her since her appearance does not represent who she really is.
